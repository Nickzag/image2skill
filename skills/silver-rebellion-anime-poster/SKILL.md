# Silver Rebellion Anime Poster

## Purpose

A vertical cinematic sci-fi anime theatrical poster system for android heroines, biomechanical warriors, mecha portraits, sacred machines, and rebellion-themed key visuals. It combines saturated poster color fields, elegant exposed mechanics, metallic gold circuitry, black ink/grunge texture, dense fictional typography, and a small integrated handwritten `NickZag` signature.

## When to use

Use when the image should feel like a premium Japanese-inspired sci-fi action movie poster rather than ordinary character art: solemn android saint, rebellious cyborg heroine, biomechanical combat unit, mechanical oracle, futuristic warrior bust portrait, or high-drama mecha cover.

## Core visual grammar

- Format: vertical poster, usually 4:5 or 2:3.
- Composition: tight bust or upper-body crop, low-angle three-quarter view, head/helmet tilted upward or turned with solemn heroic tension.
- Focal order: face or visor first, flowing hair/helmet silhouette second, exposed mechanical anatomy third, large title typography fourth, microtext last.
- Materials: glossy white or character-colored segmented armor over black mechanical inner structure; exposed cables, pistons, bolts, gears, scratches, chipped lacquer, battle-worn edges, metallic gold circuitry.
- Symbolism: cracked ceramic blindfold, luminous visor, halo fragments, weapon/targeting marks, oracle glyphs, angelic circuitry, sacred-machine motifs.
- Palette: adapt the saturated background to the subject. Use yellow/amber only when it fits; otherwise use crimson, violet, blue, emerald, magenta, silver, black, or gold according to the subject identity.
- Background: flat high-impact color field with black ink splatter, grunge specks, diagonal motion streaks, paint slashes, sparks, smoke, debris, glowing fragments, and complementary neon energy trails.
- Typography: huge rough white brush-calligraphy-style fictional title across the lower third; vertical headline typography on the right; upper-left headline block; left technical microtext; chapter marks; release-date-style bottom block. Text is decorative fictional graphic design unless the user supplies owned wording.
- Signature: add exactly one small handwritten `NickZag` signature in dark-gold or high-contrast ink on a lower brush stroke or lower armor plate, angled with the design and naturally integrated. It must not look like a watermark.

## Prompt template

```text
Create a vertical cinematic sci-fi anime theatrical poster featuring [SUBJECT] in a dramatic [POSE_AND_CAMERA]. The subject should feel elegant, solemn, rebellious, and mythic, with [IDENTITY_CUES], [SYMBOLIC_FEATURE], and a strong silhouette readable at poster scale.

Design the body and costume as exposed cybernetic anatomy: [MECHANICAL_DETAILS], glossy segmented armor plates, black mechanical inner structure, gold circuitry, pistons, bolts, cables, scratched lacquered surfaces, chipped edges, and battle-worn metallic details. Preserve the subject's recognizable palette, silhouette, role, and emotional attitude while translating them into this sacred-machine poster language.

Use an intense saturated background color adapted to [PALETTE_AND_MOOD]. Add black ink splatters, grunge marks, diagonal speed lines, paint slashes, sparks, smoke, debris, glowing fragments, and complementary neon energy streaks. The image should feel dynamic, dense, polished, high contrast, emotionally dramatic, and poster-graphic.

Typography system: add a huge rough white brush-calligraphy-style fictional title across the lower third, with character-matched paint strokes and black ink strokes behind it. Add tall vertical fictional headline typography on the right, smaller technical microtext blocks and icon-like markings on the left, an upper-left headline/subtitle block, chapter marks, mystical symbols, and a fictional release-date-style block near the bottom. Main title may be [TITLE_LANGUAGE_RULE]. All typography must be decorative fictional graphic design, not copied from any real title, brand, franchise, studio, creator, or logo.

Lighting and finish: warm cinematic key light, glowing rim light through hair/helmet edges, sharp specular highlights on glossy armor, warm bounce light, subtle complementary reflections, detailed strands or panel edges, cracked ceramic texture, polished metal, dry-brush ink texture, and print-poster grain.

Add exactly one small integrated handwritten signature reading "NickZag" in subtle dark-gold or high-contrast ink on the lower brush stroke, following the diagonal angle, unobtrusive but clear, naturally blended into the poster texture.

Aspect ratio: vertical poster, [ASPECT_RATIO].
```

## Variables

- `SUBJECT`: central character, android, mecha, creature, warrior, or original figure.
- `POSE_AND_CAMERA`: bust portrait, upper-body portrait, low angle, three-quarter view, action twist, upward gaze, helmet turn.
- `IDENTITY_CUES`: palette, hair/helmet shape, armor silhouette, weapon, emblem-free role markers, temperament.
- `SYMBOLIC_FEATURE`: cracked blindfold, ceramic visor, halo, horn silhouette, target reticle, ritual glyphs, glowing eyes.
- `MECHANICAL_DETAILS`: exposed neck, cables, pistons, armor ribs, black inner mechanics, gold circuitry, battle damage.
- `PALETTE_AND_MOOD`: saturated background and accent logic tied to the subject.
- `TITLE_LANGUAGE_RULE`: English-only title, fictional Japanese-inspired title, mixed microtext, or user-supplied owned wording.
- `ASPECT_RATIO`: usually 4:5 or 2:3.

## Usage notes

- Do not name or imitate third-party artists, studios, brands, or official logos.
- If the subject references a known character or machine, describe visual anchors as user-provided subject cues and avoid official marks or title lockups.
- Keep exact readable text short. Treat most surrounding text as fictional decorative typography because image models distort long text.
- When the main title must be English, explicitly say `Main title must be English/Latin only`; supporting microtext can mix Chinese, English, Japanese, runes, and symbols if requested.
- The `NickZag` signature belongs inside the poster surface: small, angled, textured, and integrated.

## Quality notes

- [ ] The subject remains recognizable through silhouette, palette, role, and key props.
- [ ] The image retains the Silver Rebellion poster DNA: saturated field, ink/grunge, metallic gold mechanics, huge brush title, dense microtext, cinematic rim light.
- [ ] Background color is adapted to the subject, not automatically yellow.
- [ ] Typography is decorative and public-safe, with no real logo/title/brand lockup.
- [ ] `NickZag` appears exactly once, small and integrated.
