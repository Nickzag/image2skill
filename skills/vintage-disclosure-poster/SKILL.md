# Vintage Disclosure Poster

## Purpose

A dense vintage political/editorial poster system for public disclosure, state secrecy, diplomatic spectacle, archives, bureaucracy, institutional uncertainty, and citizen analysis. The style combines aged newsprint, hand-inked caricature, muted watercolor, distressed headline typography, paper chaos, red stamps, official-looking clutter, and public-facing satire.

## When to use

Use for GPT Image 2 prompts about political events, public records, UFO/UAP or disclosure themes, diplomatic visits, hearings, data releases, bureaucracy, geopolitical theater, institutional uncertainty, and media/public analysis scenes.

Do not use for clean corporate infographics, photorealistic news photography, exact agency-logo reproduction, or prompts that depend on third-party artist names, studio names, brand marks, or creator references.

## Core visual grammar

- Build the scene as a poster/editorial cartoon, not a realistic photo.
- Use concrete visual mechanics: heavy black ink contours, caricature faces, dense cross-hatching, dry-brush shadows, rubber-stamp marks, worn cream paper, smudges, fold lines, scuffed edges, and uneven print coverage.
- Keep major typography short and structural: one headline, one subtitle, one slogan/stamp/ribbon, and at most one speech bubble.
- Use generic labels and fictional official-looking marks instead of real logos.
- Include evidence objects that explain the subject: folders, route maps, departure boards, public records, tariffs, charts, suitcases, podium notes, microphones, cameras, laptops, notebooks, tea tables, hearing desks, or archive boxes.
- Add public observers as active interpreters: journalists, citizen analysts, researchers, students, bloggers, civic technologists, photographers, and note-takers.
- Use rust red as the eye-guide: carpet, ribbon, stamp, curtain, warning label, lantern, or accent stars.
- Include a small handwritten `NickZag` signature only as an in-scene marginal note, document-corner signature, or poster-bottom scribble. It must be subtle, legible, and natural; never a watermark or logo.

## Composition system

Rotate composition deliberately. Do not repeat the same top-headline / centered-object / left-right factions / bottom-crowd structure across a series.

Suggested layout archetypes:

- `diagonal procession`: a red carpet, paper trail, route map, convoy, flight path, or parade cuts from lower-left to upper-right.
- `map-room strategy wall`: a large map, chalkboard, route board, treaty wall, or evidence wall fills the background.
- `foreground evidence table`: documents, devices, maps, and cups dominate the foreground; officials and crowds are smaller behind it.
- `circular hearing`: characters surround a round hearing table, tribunal floor, or debate arena.
- `triptych poster`: three panels show setup, conflict, and consequence.
- `tower cross-section`: a vertical bureaucracy, server, archive, aircraft, or institutional machine contains small narrative compartments.
- `street/public square`: the issue unfolds in an airport, plaza, checkpoint, market, expo hall, or press scrum.
- `surreal scale satire`: one oversized symbolic object dominates the scene, such as a giant suitcase, teacup, handshake, gavel, chip, eye, or aircraft stair.

## Prompt template

```text
Create a vertical satirical political editorial poster about [SUBJECT]. Use [COMPOSITION ARCHETYPE], not a centered black-box layout. The image should feel like a dense vintage propaganda-poster / newspaper editorial cartoon printed on old cream paper.

Main scene: [PRIMARY ACTION AND SETTING]. Show [MAIN CHARACTER OR INSTITUTIONAL FORCE] with [KEY PROPS], surrounded by [OFFICIALS / PRESS / ANALYSTS / CITIZENS]. Add theme-specific evidence objects: [DOCUMENTS, MAPS, CHARTS, LUGGAGE, ARCHIVE BOXES, ROUTE BOARDS, PODIUMS, CAMERAS, ETC.].

Typography: huge distressed black serif headline reading “[HEADLINE]”. Smaller bulletin subtitle reading “[SUBTITLE]”. Add one short slogan, stamp, ribbon, sign, or speech bubble reading “[SHORT TEXT]”. Keep all other labels short, fictional, and theme-relevant.

Rendering style: hand-drawn ink and muted watercolor editorial cartoon, heavy black contour lines, exaggerated caricature faces, dense cross-hatching, dry-brush shadows, rubber-stamp textures, distressed serif lettering, imperfect hand-lettered bubbles, worn poster grain, smudges, creases, scuffed paper edges, uneven ink coverage.

Palette: aged cream paper, charcoal black ink, faded blue-gray, olive green, dusty navy, warm document whites, muted gold, brown shadows, and rust-red accents.

Include a very small handwritten signature reading “NickZag” in black ink on a lower-right loose document or poster margin, subtle but clearly legible, matching the hand-drawn ink style; not a watermark, not a logo, not a detached overlay.

No photorealism, no glossy 3D, no clean vector art, no corporate infographic style, no exact official agency logos, no real brand logos, no third-party creator names, no studio names, no artist names, no unrelated decorative props.
```

## Variables

- `SUBJECT`: the disclosure, diplomatic event, public-data conflict, investigation, hearing, visit, archive, or institution.
- `COMPOSITION ARCHETYPE`: choose one from the composition system.
- `PRIMARY ACTION AND SETTING`: what is happening and where.
- `MAIN CHARACTER OR INSTITUTIONAL FORCE`: public figure type, officials, agency, host, delegation, scientist, analyst, or generic authority.
- `KEY PROPS`: aircraft, suitcase, podium, black archive box, report spill, route board, treaty table, maps, microphones.
- `HEADLINE`: short poster headline.
- `SUBTITLE`: one-line framing phrase.
- `SHORT TEXT`: speech bubble, stamp, label, or ribbon phrase.
- `PALETTE`: optional subject-specific accent colors while preserving the vintage base.

## Quality notes

- [ ] The composition archetype is stated explicitly.
- [ ] The prompt does not use third-party creator, studio, artist, or brand references.
- [ ] The subject can change while the visual grammar remains reusable.
- [ ] Major text is short enough for image generation.
- [ ] Officials, journalists, citizens, documents, and symbolic props support the same story.
- [ ] `NickZag` is integrated as a tiny natural handwritten signature, not a watermark.
