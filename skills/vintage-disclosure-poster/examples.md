# Vintage Disclosure Poster — Examples

## Happy presidential aircraft visit

```text
Create a vertical satirical political editorial poster about a cheerful U.S. presidential visit to China aboard a large official aircraft. Use a diagonal travel-route procession, not a centered black-box layout. The aircraft stairs and departure scene lead diagonally into the China arrival scene. A happy Trump-like caricature steps from a blue-and-white presidential-looking airplane, smiling broadly and waving while carrying a folder. Around him are luggage, press, aides, microphones, cameras, travel paperwork, and security figures. A red carpet or route line guides the eye toward a welcoming China scene with a ceremonial gate, red lanterns, officials, photographers, a tea table, and a host figure receiving the delegation. Huge distressed black serif headline: “HEADED TO CHINA”. Subtitle: “BIG SMILES, BIG OPTICS, BIG EXPECTATIONS”. Speech bubble: “I'M READY FOR THE BIG VISIT”. Hand-drawn ink and muted watercolor editorial cartoon, aged cream newsprint, heavy black contours, dense cross-hatching, rubber-stamp texture, smudges, creases, rust-red accents, tiny handwritten “NickZag” on a lower-right document. No real logos, no brands, no third-party creator names.
```

## Public files disclosure

```text
Vertical satirical political editorial poster about public evidence files being released after years of secrecy. Use a central archive reveal: a giant black filing cabinet opens and spills reports, diagrams, red stamps, maps, and case folders toward a crowd of citizen analysts. Headline “THE FILES ARE OPEN”. Subtitle “NO CLEARANCE REQUIRED”. Rust-red stamp “YOU DECIDE”. Show pompous officials, a confused scientist, journalists, laptops, cameras, notebooks, and public observers actively reading the evidence. Hand-drawn ink, muted watercolor, distressed serif type, aged cream paper, faded blue-gray shadows, olive and dusty navy clothing, rust-red accents, tiny handwritten “NickZag” on a loose foreground document. No exact official logos, no brands, no artist names.
```
